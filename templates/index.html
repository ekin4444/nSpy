How to Use

Follow these simple steps to upload your MRI images and receive preliminary analysis results, helping you take the first step toward understanding your brain health.

  • 1) Go to the Homepage
  • 2) Navigate to the Detection Page
  • 3) Upload Your MRI Image
  • 4) Submit for Analysis

Go to the Homepage

Start by visiting the NeuroSpy homepage and logging in to your account. If you don’t have an account, sign up easily using your email.

Navigate to the Detection Page

After logging in, find and click on the “Detection” tab in the main menu or homepage. This will take you to the detection page.

Upload Your MRI Image

On the detection page, click the “Upload MRI” button. Select a front-facing, black MRI image of your brain from your device and confirm your upload.

Submit for Analysis

Once your image is uploaded, click the “Submit” button to start the analysis. You’ll receive a notification when the results are ready—remember to consult a professional for further guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to our FAQ section! Here, we address some of the most common questions about NeuroSpy, how it works, and what you can expect from our platform. Whether you're curious about data security, the analysis process, or available resources, you’ll find helpful answers to guide you in using NeuroSpy effectively. If you have any additional questions, feel free to reach out!

  • How does NeuroSpy work?

    NeuroSpy allows users to upload MRI images, which are then analyzed using advanced machine learning algorithms to detect potential brain tumors. Results are provided for guidance, but professional medical consultation is essential for a definitive diagnosis.

  • What should I do with the results?

    If you receive a notification of potential issues, it’s important to consult a healthcare professional for further evaluation and diagnosis. NeuroSpy is designed to assist, not replace, medical advice.

  • Is there a cost to use NeuroSpy?

    NeuroSpy is designed to be accessible, and we aim to provide resources and tools at no cost. Please check the website for any specific features that may require payment or donations for research support.

  • What resources does NeuroSpy provide?

    In addition to detection, NeuroSpy offers an article bank on brain cancer, self-assessment tools, educational courses, and links to fundraising organizations that support brain cancer research.

  • What type of MRI image should I upload?

    Users should upload a front-facing MRI image of their brain, ensuring it is in black format for optimal analysis by the system.
